# Images: Stars from Adolescence to Old Age

## Figure References

### [FIGURE 1: The Ant Nebula]
A planetary nebula showing rings of gas ejected from a dying star, glowing orange-red from UV radiation of hot white dwarf at center. Double-lobed structure suggests multiple mass-loss episodes or viewing through doughnut-shaped torus. *Context: Chapter opening, illustrating stellar death.*

### [FIGURE 2: Star Layers During Shell Burning]
Cross-section of red giant: contracting He core (center, hottest), H-fusion shell (orange), inert H layer, expanded cool outer envelope. Color indicates temperature gradient. *Context: Concept 2, explaining core contraction vs. envelope expansion.*

### [FIGURE 3: Relative Sizes of Stars]
Comparative diagram: Sun as small yellow sphere; Betelgeuse as vast red sphere many radii larger. Earth's orbit shown for scale, lies inside Betelgeuse's photosphere. *Context: Concept 2, making red-giant expansion visceral.*

### [FIGURE 4: Betelgeuse]
Photograph showing orange-red supergiant in Orion constellation, marking hunter's shoulder. *Context: Named star showing actual red supergiant; connects abstract concepts to observable objects.*

### [FIGURE 5: Evolutionary Tracks (H-R Diagram)]
H-R diagram with main sequence and curved evolutionary tracks for stars M = 0.5–15 solar masses. Tracks labeled with ages at key points; show rapid evolution off main sequence for massive stars. *Context: Concept 2 & 4, visualizing lifetimes and mass dependence.*

### [FIGURE 6: Omega Centauri (Globular Cluster)]
Dense spherical concentration of hundreds of thousands of stars, brighter stars are orange-red giants. *Context: Concept 4, showing globular cluster structure and evolved stellar population.*

### [FIGURE 7: Jewel Box (NGC 4755)]
Open cluster with loosely scattered stars, many bright blue (young, massive) stars visible. *Context: Concept 4, contrasting with globular cluster; shows open cluster looseness and youth.*

### [FIGURE 8: Pleiades]
Wide-field image of Pleiades ("Seven Sisters"), bright stars visible to naked eye in Taurus. Appears blue due to scattered light and youth of stars. *Context: Famous naked-eye cluster, ~100 Myr old.*

### [FIGURE 9: Young Cluster H-R Diagram (NGC 2264)]
H-R diagram: main sequence (bright stars, upper left), pre-main-sequence stars (above/right), Hayashi track showing contraction path. Age ~3 Myr. *Context: Concept 4, showing young cluster structure.*

### [FIGURE 10: NGC 3293]
Photograph of ~10 Myr old cluster; gas/dust dispersed, one bright orange-red star (red giant) stands out among fainter main-sequence stars. *Context: Intermediate-age cluster, some stars have evolved off main sequence.*

### [FIGURE 11: Cluster M41 H-R Diagram]
H-R diagram showing main sequence, noticeable gap (stars evolving rapidly through it), significant red-giant population. Age ~100 Myr. Turnoff luminosity indicates age. *Context: Intermediate-age cluster; introduces main-sequence turnoff concept.*

### [FIGURE 12: 47 Tucanae H-R Diagram]
H-R diagram with **compressed luminosity scale** (much smaller range than younger clusters). Very faint main sequence, short turnoff at low luminosity. Age >11 Gyr. *Context: Globular cluster; demonstrates extreme age through sparse main sequence.*

### [FIGURE 13: Eta Carinae]
Photograph of very massive star surrounded by expanding shells of ejected gas and nebulosity. Central point source often hidden by surrounding material. *Context: Concept 5, showing massive star's violent life and strong stellar winds.*

### [FIGURE 14: Interior Structure of Massive Star]
Cross-section showing concentric shells: Fe core (center), Si-fusing shell, O-fusing shell, Ne-fusing shell, C-fusing shell, He-fusing shell, H-fusing shell, outer H envelope. Each labeled with temperature. *Context: Concept 5, visualizing nucleosynthesis onion structure.*

### [FIGURE 15: Planetary Nebula Gallery]
Composite of 4–6 Hubble images of different planetary nebulae (Ring, Helix, Butterfly, etc.). Show diverse shapes: rings, dumbbells, knots, bipolar lobes. Colors: red (H-alpha), green (O III), blue (He). *Context: Concept 5 & Integration, demonstrating beauty and diversity of stellar death products.*

### [FIGURE 16: Planetary Nebula Shape Model]
Diagram showing dense doughnut (torus) disk around central dying star, with material flowing outward along rotation axis. Different viewing angles illustrated: edge-on (ring), polar (lobes + disk), intermediate (complex). *Context: Concept 5, explaining why same physics produces different morphologies depending on viewing angle.*

### [FIGURE 17: Sun-Mass Star H-R Evolution]
H-R diagram with marked evolutionary track for 1 M_sun star: zero-age main sequence (yellow dot), long drift on MS, rapid movement to upper right (red giant), brief excursion left after He flash, return to giant region, then final death. *Context: Integration, showing complete lifetime path graphically.*
