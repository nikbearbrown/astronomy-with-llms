## Image Sourcing Notes

Primary sources for images in converted chapter:

- **Voyager 1 & 2 raw images**: PDS Planetary Rings Node and JPL archives
- **Galileo spacecraft**: Galileo SSI (Solid-State Imaging) archives, JPL
- **Cassini spacecraft**: Cassini CICLOPS team, JPL/ESA

- **Juno spacecraft**: Juno CAM data, NASA

All images should be credited to their respective missions. Where copyright permits, images are in public domain (NASA images). Where specific attribution is required, consult original mission data archives.

## Image Production Notes for Future Editions

1. Diagrams should use consistent color coding: water ice = blue, rock/silicate = brown, organic/carbon compounds = dark gray/black, sulfur compounds = yellow/orange.

2. Scale bars and distance indicators should be prominent on all planetary surface images.

3. Orbital diagrams should use animation-friendly frame sequences for clarity in different media.

4. Thermal/false-color images (Enceladus, Titan radar) should include legends explaining color scale and units.

5. Comparative size images should maintain accurate relative sizes rather than artistic freedom.
