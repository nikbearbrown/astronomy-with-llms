# Bookmap: Giant Planets Chapter Conversion Analysis

**Source:** OpenStax Astronomy Chapter 11 (Sections on Composition, Structure, Internal Heat, Magnetic Fields, Atmospheres, Weather, Storms)

**Conversion:** Attenborough × Feynman v1.1 style

---

## What the Source Material Teaches

The source text provides:

1. **Comprehensive compositional inventory** — detailed table of abundances (H2 75%, He 24%, traces of water, methane, ammonia, rocks). This grounds the discussion of formation.

2. **Historical spacecraft narrative** — Pioneer, Voyager, Galileo, Cassini, Juno missions spanning 1973–2016. Rich material for cold opens and historical context.

3. **Physical parameter tables** — Jovian planet distances, periods, diameters, masses, densities. Essential for understanding why planets differ.

4. **Interior structure explanation** — Rocky cores, liquid hydrogen mantles, metallic hydrogen regions, pressure estimates. The mechanism chapter relies entirely on this.

5. **Atmospheric chemistry and cloud structure** — Ammonia clouds, methane absorption, photochemistry. Less detailed in the conversion but present.

6. **Atmospheric circulation and weather patterns** — Wind speed diagrams, cloud bands, zonal flows. Provides data for the atmosphere chapter.

7. **Storm systems and timescales** — Great Red Spot 300-year persistence, dimensions, rotation periods. The worked example for Concept 3 draws directly from this.

8. **Magnetic fields and magnetospheres** — Synchrotron radiation discovery, magnetosphere size, magnetic tilts. Touched on but not deeply developed in the conversion.

9. **James Van Allen biography** — Historical sidebar providing human context for magnetosphere research. Used minimally in conversion.

## Structural Decisions Made

**What the source doesn't emphasize, the conversion highlights:**

1. **Formation as outcome, not accident** — Source lists abundances; conversion explains *why* these abundances matter for planet-building.

2. **Pressure as the bridge** — Source treats interior structure separately from other properties; conversion makes pressure the causal link from mass to structure to heat source.

3. **Storm persistence as fundamental** — Source describes Great Red Spot; conversion explains *why* it persists (no friction) vs. Earth hurricanes.

4. **Scale as a cascade** — Source presents composition, structure, atmosphere as independent sections; conversion shows them as consequences of initial distance from Sun.

**What the source emphasizes, the conversion downplays:**

1. **Magnetic field details** — Source has substantial section on magnetospheres and synchrotron radiation. Conversion mentions magnetic fields briefly; full exploration deferred to connections forward.

2. **Spacecraft engineering triumphs** — Source celebrates Voyager 2's achievement (arthritic arm, failing memory). Conversion uses Voyager narrative for opening scene, not for engineering highlight.

3. **Van Allen biography** — Source includes historical sidebar. Conversion omits in favor of staying focused on concepts.

4. **Atmospheric chemistry specifics** — Source lists hydrocarbons, photochemical reactions, color uncertainty. Conversion mentions photochemistry in misconception but doesn't dwell on details.

## Fidelity and Compression

**Content fidelity check:**

- Composition tables: Converted to prose narrative (frost line, capture mechanics) rather than tabular
- Interior structure: Simplified from 3-layer model (gas/liquid/metallic) to conceptual explanation of pressure-driven phase transition
- Storm data: Great Red Spot dimensions and rotation period preserved for worked example
- Wind speeds: Concept 3 references Jupiter 90 m/s equatorial jet and Neptune supersonic speeds from source
- Planet masses: Conversion uses "318 Earths" (Jupiter), "95 times Earth" (Saturn), "15 times Earth" (Uranus/Neptune) — all from source

**Compression necessary because:**

Source text is ~10,500 words across five major sections. Conversion is ~7,000 words, eight sections (including learning objectives and connections). Compression achieved by:

1. Merging "Composition" and "Exploration" into a single Concept 1 (formation drives current composition)
2. Consolidating "Internal Heat" into Concept 2 (pressure as mechanism)
3. Consolidating "Magnetic Fields" reference into Concept 3 (mentioned but not detailed)
4. Cutting "Collaborative Group Activities" and "Review Questions" (those are separate outputs)

**No fidelity loss in core mechanisms:**

- Frost line as compositional boundary: Preserved
- Gravity capture of gas by massive cores: Preserved
- Hydrogen phase transitions under pressure: Preserved
- Internal heat sources (primordial and helium rain): Preserved
- Storm persistence without friction: Preserved
- Zonal wind organization by rotation: Preserved

## Voice Calibration

**Attenborough × Feynman register achieved through:**

1. **Present-tense scene opening** — Voyager 2 approaching Neptune uses "you are" and active verbs
2. **First-person pedagogical voice** — "Let us step back," "Here is where the weather becomes different"
3. **Mechanism revealed from first principles** — Hydrogen pressure descent explained without jargon
4. **Named trade-offs** — Stated as "the cost" and "the benefit," not hidden
5. **Scale oscillation** — From hydrogen molecule to planetary systems to exoplanet diversity
6. **Moral weight via accumulation** — Each concept builds to "planets are not designed but emerged"
7. **Ear test** — Sections read aloud for rhythm and clarity

✗ "Fascinating," "remarkable," "interestingly"
✗ "Obviously," "clearly"
✗ "It is worth noting"
✗ "One could argue"

✓ "Here is where the weather becomes different"
