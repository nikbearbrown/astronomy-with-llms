# Figures & Image Descriptions for Chapter 29: The Big Bang

## Figure 1: Planck Satellite CMB All-Sky Map

**Citation:** Planck Collaboration (2018). ESA/Planck Collaboration.

**Description:** All-sky map of the cosmic microwave background showing temperature fluctuations. The map is presented in Mollweide projection with the Milky Way emission masked out (shown as a white band across the center). Color scale represents temperature deviations of roughly ±0.0001 K from the mean of 2.73 K. Red regions are slightly hotter, blue regions slightly cooler. The fluctuations appear random to the eye but contain structure visible in the power spectrum (acoustic peaks visible in inset graph).

**Inset:** Angular power spectrum showing intensity of temperature variations as a function of angular scale on the sky. The characteristic peaks at specific angular scales represent frozen sound waves from the early universe plasma. First peak indicates spatial flatness; second and third peaks reveal baryon and dark matter densities.

**Visual:** Total width ~8 inches for textbook integration. Inset power spectrum graph ~3 inches wide, placed in upper right corner.

**Alternative description for accessibility:** The full-sky map looks like a mottled texture; regions of slightly higher and lower temperature create a fine-grained pattern across the entire celestial sphere. The accompanying graph shows a series of distinct peaks and valleys, with the first peak being the tallest, occurring at an angular scale of about 1 degree.

## Figure 2: Timeline of the Big Bang

**Citation:** Original figure based on standard Big Bang model. Timeline data from Kolb & Turner (1990), Planck results.

**Description:** A vertical timeline showing the history of the universe from $t = 10^{-43}$ seconds (Planck time) to the present (13.8 billion years). The timeline is logarithmic in time, with major transitions marked.

**Key events labeled (top to bottom):**
- $t = 10^{-43}$ s: Planck time (no theory available)
- $t = 10^{-12}$ s: Quark-gluon plasma phase transition; quarks form protons and neutrons
- $t = 10^{-6}$ s: Electron-positron annihilation nearly complete
- $t = 1$ s: Neutron-to-proton ratio freezes at 1:6
- $t = 3$ s: Big Bang nucleosynthesis ends; He and H abundances frozen
- $t = 50,000$ years: Matter-radiation equality (universe becomes matter-dominated)
- $t = 380,000$ years: Recombination; universe becomes transparent (CMB released)
- $t = 100$ million years: First stars begin to form
- $t = 13.8$ billion years: Present day

**Right side of figure:** Temperature and density scales (logarithmic).
- At $t = 10^{-43}$ s: T = $10^{32}$ K, density = Planck density
- At $t = 1$ s: T = 10^10 K, density ~ nuclear density
- At $t = 380,000$ years: T = 3,000 K, density ~ 10^-3 times present
- At $t = 13.8$ Gyr: T = 2.73 K (CMB temperature today), average density ~ $10^{-26}$ kg/m³

**Visual:** Vertical orientation, approximately 6 inches tall × 3 inches wide for textbook. Color-coded regions: purple for first second (strong interactions dominate), blue for first 380,000 years (radiation era), green from 380,000 years to ~100 million years (transition to matter era), orange from ~100 million years to present (matter and structure).

## Figure 3: Hubble's Relation and Age Estimation (optional advanced)

**Description:** Two-panel figure. Left panel: distance vs. recession velocity scatter plot for nearby galaxies. Right panel: illustration of running the expansion "backward" in time to converge at a single point (the Big Bang).

**Left panel:** Scatter plot with distance (megaparsecs) on horizontal axis, recession velocity (km/s) on vertical axis. Data points for ~20-30 galaxies scattered roughly along a straight line passing through the origin. Linear fit shown as a line. Slope is Hubble constant (~70 km/s/Mpc).

**Right panel:** Cartoon showing expanding universe (three circles of increasing size, representing universe at three different times). Below each circle, an arrow pointing backward in time converges to a single point at age zero. Caption: "Running the expansion backward: all matter converges to a point ~13.8 billion years ago."

## Image Notes for Production

All figures use LaTeX rendering for axes, labels, and equations. Color schemes are colorblind-friendly (red-blue diverging for temperature maps, using standard astronomical convention).

The CMB map (Figure 1) should be high-resolution (>300 dpi) for print. The timeline (Figure 2) can be vectorized for crisp rendering at any scale.

No photographs required. All figures are either observational data products (CMB map from public Planck archives) or schematic diagrams (timeline, Hubble relation).
