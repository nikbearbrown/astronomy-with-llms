### Section 2: Habitable Zones

**[FIGURE: Habitable Zone Around Sun-Like Star]**
- Placement: In "The Problem of Goldilocks" section
- Caption: "The habitable zone is the region around a star where a planet could maintain liquid water on its surface. For the Sun, this zone includes Earth but excludes the too-hot Venus and too-cold Mars."
- Source: Likely OSC_Astro_30_03_* (habitable zones section)
- Notes: Diagram showing Sun with orbital distances, colored bands for habitable zone, planetary positions

**[FIGURE: Venus, Earth, Mars Comparison - Atmosphere and Temperature]**
- Placement: In "The Problem of Goldilocks" section, after greenhouse effect explanation
- Caption: "Venus and Mars demonstrate how atmospheric composition controls surface temperature. Venus's thick CO₂ atmosphere traps heat (runaway greenhouse). Mars's thin atmosphere cannot retain heat. Earth's moderate atmosphere maintains habitability."
- Source: OSC_Astro_30_01_Comet and comparative planetary data
- Notes: Three planetary disks with atmospheric composition graphs, temperature overlays

**[FIGURE: Habitable Zone Migration Over Star's Lifetime]**
- Placement: In "The Problem of Goldilocks" section, after discussion of Sun brightening
- Caption: "As stars age, their luminosity increases. The habitable zone migrates outward. Four billion years ago, the Sun was dimmer; Earth was at a different orbital distance relative to the habitable zone than it is now."

- Notes: Time series showing habitable zone position at different stellar ages

**[FIGURE: Europa - Ice Crust and Subsurface Ocean]**
